AFL Arena Reference Pack v1: Crash Site Omega
Purpose

This reference pack consolidates the key visual and structural guidelines for Crash Site Omega, the first AFL arena. It serves as the source of truth for level designers, artists, and AI tool chains when building or adjusting the map. Each section outlines what the arena should look like and how it should be read in play.

Components
Hero Key Art

A cinematic hero shot that shows the entire arena in its televised, broadcast‐ready state. The shot should depict:

A wrecked spacecraft hull dominating the center lane.
Broken satellites, turbine housings, and industrial debris creating cover clusters.
Flanking routes to the left and right with lighter cover and clear lines of movement.
The objective “uplink tower” glowing in the distance as the match goal.
Holographic score pylons, digital banners, and AFL branding integrated into the scene.
Smoke, sparks, and floodlights to evoke a premium sports‐combat atmosphere.

Use this key art for investor decks and mood boards; it sets the tone for the entire arena.

Top‑Down Tactical Map

A clear overhead diagram of the arena showing:

Deployment bays on each end, with the robot spawn tunnels and launch gates.
A wide central assault lane leading directly to the objective.
Left and right flank lanes that wrap around the midfield wreck zone.
The midfield wreck zone itself, with a satellite spine landmark, collapsed catwalk, and turbine debris cluster.
Cover clusters, hazard props and rotation points.
The objective core at the far end, with its capture zone radius.

This map is crucial for level layout decisions, AI routeing and collision planning.

Builder Callout Board

An orthographic breakdown identifying the arena’s major landmarks and sectors:

Deployment Bay Alpha/Omega: spawn tunnels, launch gates, faction branding.
Central Assault Lane: main path, heavy cover and crash props.
Left/Right Flank Lanes: secondary routes with lighter cover and flanking angles.
Midfield Wreck Zone: crashed satellites, torn hulls, catwalk fragments, turbine debris.
Elevated Tactical Positions: limited catwalks and towers for vertical play.
Objective Core: the uplink tower, score ring and contest barrier.
Hazard Props: steam vents, sparking conduits, broken drones and antenna towers.

Each callout should include scale references and notes on collision volumes.

Gameplay Route Overlay

This overlay adds arrows and pressure zones to the top‑down map, illustrating expected player and AI flow:

Primary flow from spawn through lanes → midfield → objective.
Secondary flanking routes for repositioning and recovery.
Contest hotspots at the first collision point, midfield pivot and objective barrier.
Pressure arrows showing how momentum should build toward the objective.

Use this overlay to evaluate whether cover placement and sightlines support the intended sports loop.

Spawn & Deployment Zone Detail Sheet

Focuses on the staging areas on each end of the map. It should show:

The design of the spawn tunnels and launch gates.
Placement of faction logos and digital signage.
Warmup platforms or ramps leading into the play space.
Lighting, smoke and FX cues for robot entry moments.

These details ensure the deployment sequence feels like a televised sports introduction.

Objective Zone Detail Sheet

Breaks down the scoring area:

The uplink tower design (energy relay, uplink antenna, or reactor core).
Capture ring radius and height variations.
Surrounding cover for attackers and defenders.
Visual indicators for capture progress (lights, holographic progress bars, scoreboard pings).

Clarity here is essential; spectators and players must instantly recognise the objective and its status.

Midfield & Landmark Detail Sheet

Explains the design and placement of central props:

Crashed Ship Hull – a massive torn hull that defines the center lane.
Satellite Spine – a long, curved structure that splits lanes and provides cover.
Collapsed Catwalk – a vertical element that hints at the arena’s history.
Turbine Cluster – mechanical wreckage that creates cover clusters.
Broken Drones & Antennas – smaller props to support cover and visual storytelling.

These details help maintain consistent scale and story across the arena.

Design Rules Recap
Macro before micro: build the play space and flow before adding dressing.
Clear lane identity: center lane = high pressure; side lanes = flanking and recovery.
Visual readability: avoid prop clutter; ensure strong silhouettes and distinct landmarks.
Objective clarity: the scoring zone must stand out visually and remain visible from most of the map.
Broadcast framing: every landmark should support hero shots, overhead sweeps and climax camera angles.
Robotics sport identity: integrate AFL branding, telemetry screens, and high‑tech props to reinforce the theme.

This reference pack should accompany the Crash Site Omega build kit and gameplay logic board, providing visual context for all design and implementation decisions.
